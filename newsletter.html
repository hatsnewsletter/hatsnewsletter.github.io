A(bstract)typography

Atypography, or abstract typography, is the concept of deconstructing letters into abstract shapes with intention. It creates a deliberate barrier between text and the artwork it accompanies, acting as a form of friction. This approach elevates the form of the artwork while still allowing the message to be conveyed.

DIY Janko Piano

A series of videos in which Mike Filip attempts to build his own piano with a Janko layout, in which the keys are arranged such that you can play the same melody in different keys without changing your hand shape, similar to how it is on a guitar. In the videos there are some interesting discussions of how different piano key mechanisms work.

S1: The $6 R1 Competitor?

A summary of the S1 paper by Tim Kellogg, which is about a small chain-of-thought language model akin to OpenAI’s o1 and Deepseek R1. This is a much smaller model trained with slightly different methods compared to the big shots, but the goals are the same: to make the language model write out some of its thoughts before answering. I don’t know whether to find the method they used to keep the model thinking for longer astonishing or disappointing.

Everyone knows your location: tracking myself down through in-app ads

Tim Sh delves deep into how apps track their users and sell data by manually investigating the network packets sent out by his phone.

Koya Bound

A travel log by Craig Mod and Dan Rubin who walked for eight days along the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage route in Japan in April 2016. Besides the lovely photography, I love the map on the side that shows you where on the route the travellers are as you scroll.
