Binary Modding a Water Dispenser

Displeased with the user experience on a new water dispenser, Robbe Derks takes matters into his own hands. He dumps, reverse engineers and modifies the firmware to get the buttons acting the way he wants. A nice example of how the reverse engineering process for firmware works.

Generate a keyboard from a hand scan

This website lets you scan your hand using a camera, and then generates you a comfortable keyboard chassis based on your measurements. Then you can print the chassis on a 3D printer and add in electronics to make a custom keyboard! There are a bunch of other configuration options ranging from which board and switches you are going to use to additional stuff like encoders.

Reverse Engineering a VanMoof e-shifter

I had heard that VanMoofs were techy bicycles but had no idea they had automatic shifters until I saw this blog post. In this series Mike Coats details the process of reverse engineering the communication protocol used by the system. The effort is still in-progress, but the tools employed so far are quite interesting.

Ribbon Mic Build

A build-log for a ribbon microphone. The author talks about how ribbon mics work and builds one from scratch. The painstaking process of corrugating a thin sheet of metal to create the ribbon is described in detail.
