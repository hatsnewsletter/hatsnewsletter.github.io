a YEAR of design to market summarized

ALIMP Design Workshop summarizes their rapid product development and low volume manufacturing process of their lamp. Has great insights on how to select, manufacture, market your own designs with a small budget.

You could have designed Positional Encoding

This article describes how positional encoding, which is the technique used to inform large language models of the word order in the input, works. Without proper positional information, the output of a model run can be the same for sequences with different word orders and meanings. Some familiarity with deep learning concepts might be required to understand the content, but the animations (made in Manim?) are nice regardless.

How to create Ditherpunk images

Surma explains what dithering is and how it’s implemented, going over the many different possible methods. The idea of adding small perturbations to break up large uniform areas is interesting.

Grab creates its own mapping service

Grab transformed their drivers into a decentralized mapping force. It hadn’t occurred to me that delivery apps were in a position to create their own maps before reading this article. Local businesses may still have some advantages over Big Tech companies when it comes to their own markets. Quite surprising that they made and used their own mapping hardware.

Subway Station Layouts

Albert Guillaumes explored and documented 1,753 metro stations across Europe, driven by a curiosity of how engineers planned and built these complex structures. During the COVID-19 lockdown, he digitized all his sketches. You can select and explore any metro station you want to see directly on the map.
