3D printing giant things with a python jigsaw generator

Cal Bryant creates a program that automatically splits big 3D models into smaller parts with jigsaw-like edges, so that they fit on a 3D printer and can be printed one by one. No projects printed using the method yet, but the development process is given in detail.

A Unique Satellite Reaction Wheel - Breaking Taps

Reaction wheels are heavy disks connected to motors. One use for them is to steer and orient satellites using the conservation of momentum. In this video Breaking Taps explains how a different design that circulates liquid metal around works and builds a few of his own. While this alternative seems mostly useful for spacecraft, it’s possible we will see these motors in other applications.

The Most Accurate Pendulum Clock on the Planet

Daniel Valuch at CERN gets his hands on a pendulum clock. He modifies it, adding in a phase locked loop to sync it to a super-precise caesium fountain clock (which they of course have), so that the old grandpa clock deviates less than a second in 158 million years. The resulting system ends up successfully correcting for vibrations from an earthquake (granted, one that’s very far away).

When Life Give You Lemons You Make Homebrew Batteries

Hans Summers attempts to power his radio transmitter with a lemon battery. He comes up with modifications to the batteries to make lemon juice radio communication possible in the blogpost which are worth a read.

Rodney Brooks’ Predictions Scorecard

Rodney Brooks, one of the founders of iRobot (of Roomba fame) and many other robotics startups, provides an annual update on his predictions related to self-driving cars, robotics, AI and space travel. This year he focuses on the developments made in self-driving cars and addresses the advancements in generative AI. Contrary to most predictions of this sort, he has a more modest assessment of the pace of technological development.
