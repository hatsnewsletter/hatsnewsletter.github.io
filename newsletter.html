The Italian Town That Built Its Own Sun

For three months every year, the Italian town of Viganella is shrouded in darkness, thanks to the surrounding mountains. In 2006, architect Giacomo Bonzani proposed a solution: a massive mirror, 8 meters wide and 5 meters tall, installed on a nearby peak. Controlled by software, the mirror reflects sunlight into the main square for 6 hours daily, bringing warmth and light. Beyond practical benefits, it fosters socialization in the town's quiet winters and since has inspired similar efforts, like the one in Rjukan Norway.

Using Erlang upgrade a drone while in-flight. - Magnus Feuer

Magnus Feuer demonstrates the hot-reloading capabilities of the programming language Erlang by updating the flight-controller code of a drone while it’s flying. Something relevant to check out is the Nerves Project, which is a framework for embedded systems running on Erlang’s infrastructure.
