The article discusses a detachable multi-fingered robotic hand designed for grasping and crawling. When detached, it can move independently, increasing its reach and showcasing its effectiveness in navigating various environments.

How labels multiply your knowledgebase, cash, time, on LOW effort. by David Malawey

David Malawey's video highlights the benefits of labels, including QR codes for easy data access and compliance with legal standards. It also showcases innovative features like smudge-free designs and color coding to enhance efficiency.

Diffusion is spectral autoregression

This blog post investigates the frequency spectra of data used to train diffusion models. This winds up creating some interesting insights into why some types of data result in higher quality output.

PiPER is a next-gen lightweight robotic arm by AgileX, available at the price of $2,499 USD

The PiPER robotic arm by AgileX, priced at $2,499, combines lightweight design with robust functionality, featuring six integrated joint motors for precise control. With a reach of 626mm and a payload capacity of 1.5kg, this compact 4.2kg arm is ideal for use in labs, classrooms, and commercial settings

Printing solder to create circuit boards

Somehow a “side-project” in a car build series, this is a 2D gantry that melts solder onto a panel of FR4 to create circuit traces. Seems to be built using a standard 3D printer nozzle and extruder. One downside is that spray glue needs to be applied to the board for the traces to stick.

ErgO Smart ring keyboard+mouse - Sophia Dai

Sophia Dai’s ErgO is a smart, Bluetooth-enabled ring replacing keyboard and mouse in a very small form-factor. It features an IMU, trackball, buttons, and device switching, ideal for everyday tasks and active use. She shares her code and has building instructions on Hackaday.

Bambu Lab A1 Mini automated with automatic plate changer kit

Andre Bar’s DIY kit Swapmod enables a Bambu Lab A1 to become almost fully automated. The printer can swap its own build plate to be able to keep printing indefinitely.

Figure 02 - a practical humanoid robot

The Czinger 21C Chassis

Czinger Vehicles’ Bio-Logic chassis is designed by optimizing multiple variables together: stiffness, weight, fatigue, crash safety, and manufacturing using AI-driven software. It is additively manufactured with their own patented alloys.

Getting my news from a dot matrix printer

Andrew Schmelyun builds a custom "front page" using a dot matrix printer (and a Raspberry Pi) to get his daily news, weather and stocks info.

Can we print a sub 1minute Benchy?

Roetz 4.0 explores whether a sub 1 minute benchy is possible with a machine that has been converted from an old CMM, air bearings and custom designed parts using Klipper Firmware.

Laser engraving 3D prints -Batch Research Lab

Using an X tool laser on 3D printed parts the team achieved the best results with a 2W infrared laser, creating white markings on black ABS and etching it. The markings are water and weather proof. A full breakdown of their process and parameters is available on their website.

RO1 Advanced Affordable 6 Axis Robot -Standard Bots

RO1 is a advanced, affordable six-axis robot for industrial use, with an 18kg payload and 0.025mm repeatability. It features an integrated 3D camera and a no-code UI for easy automation, requiring no extra controllers, making it simple to integrate into manufacturing setups.

BirdBot, efficient design inspired by birds' legs -Dynamic Locomotion Group

BirdBot is a robot that uses an avian-inspired, spring tendon system, enabling energy-efficient walking with minimal control. It reduces knee effort by 90% and uses a self-engaging mechanism for stable movement without requiring sensors. The design is scalable for larger robotic systems.

Bimetallic Tea Monitoring Device -Chronova Engineering

Chronova Engineering designs and machines a custom tea temperature monitoring device powered by a bi-metallic strip. It rings when the tea reaches the perfect temperature for drinking.

Custom Monster Chopper -Grind Hard Plumbing Co

Grind Hard Plumbing Co test rides their custom-built monster chopper with extraordinarily large wheels. The frame looks amazing!

USB-C on ANY AirPods -Exploring the Simulation

Ken Pillonel creates a mod that can be installed on any AirPod that converts them to USB-C. It has an SLS-printed enclosure and a custom flex PCB. He shares his process, and the files are open-source. His products are also available for purchase on his web shop.

Watch Dial Using Printed Circuit Board (PCB) | DIY Mechanical Watch - Part 2 : 3 Steps (with Pictures) - Instructables

Indoorgeek makes a unique mechanical watch with a CNC-machined enclosure. The dial is made from a PCB with copper hour markings and a silkscreen for the seconds.

Georg Nees: Computergrafik

Computer Graphics was the first global exhibition of algorithm-generated works, held at Siemens in Erlangen and the Studiengalerie der TH Stuttgart in February 1965. Georg Nees, the sole artist, displayed ten to twelve small graphics created by a programmed digital computer, pioneering computer art and advancing computer graphics technology.

Milking 1000 spiders to make guitar strings - Mattias Krantz

Mattias Krantz milks thousands of spiders for their silk to make a guitar using spider silk strings. He makes a loom from a bicycle, pays villagers for spiders and becomes a local legend.

"Revolutionizing Motion Retargeting for Legged Robots with Differentiable Optimal Control”

The article by Disney Research discusses a new method for copying movements from animals or animations onto robots with legs, using a technique that makes these movements more accurate and efficient. This advancement is important for improving how robots move in fields like robotics, animation, and gaming.

Creative Technologies Talks -Aalto University

The Creative Technologies Talks hosted by Aalto University offer a series of videos featuring experts from universities and organizations such as KTH, Aalto University, and Imperial College London. These presentations delve into various aspects of creative technologies, including AI in music, performance, games, and art, as well as advancements in extended reality (XR) and computational design.

Is cat purring chaotic? -Not David

Not David investigates the science of cat purring, examining whether it is a chaotic or orderly process involving muscle, nerve, and brain coordination. It highlights the diversity in purring patterns among cats, linking this to chaos theory and its prevalence in many other phenomena.

Fun command line tools by Charm

Charm creates intuitive and elegant command line tools for activities such as sending emails, reading content, storing data, and terminal applications. They offer all these tools openly for public use.

Teardown Library: a community of product designers searching for inspiration

Teardown Library is a community-driven platform offering resources for product designers, including workshops, meetups, online forums, and design challenges. It provides hands-on experience by exploring various products, fostering skill improvement and networking opportunities for designers.

picoSYNTH by Johan Peitz

Created by Johan Peitz, PicoSYNTH makes creating simple loops and beats fun. The click and drag interface enables you to combine the equipment in interesting ways making your own analog synths!

USB-C cables head-to-head teardown by Lumafield

Lumafiels has CT scanned USB-C cables currently on the market. You can view the 3D CT scan data. They present their analysis and compare them against eachother.

Carl Bugeja’s Self-Reflowing Circuit Boards

Carl Bugeja shares his design and testing process of his self-soldering circuit boards. The PCB's ground plane layer is transformed into a resistor to heat up and melt solder, enabling the circuit to self-assemble and potentially create daughter circuits.

Crafting Miniature Magic: A Deep Dive into a 1:96 Scale Flying P-51 Mustang Model

Martin Newell crafted a remarkable 1:96 scale flying P-51 Mustang model, complete with features like navigation lights, flashing cannons, and retractable landing gear, all weighing just 2.9 grams and made mostly from scratch. Viewable on his YouTube channel.

Mixing 3D Printing + Fungi for Experimental Building Materials

PLP Architecture is pioneering in construction by combining 3D printing with fungi to create eco-friendly building materials. Their method involves using wood-based PLA molds infused with mycelium bio-composites, resulting in lightweight, fire-resistant materials ideal for soundproofing and insulation.

Soviet-era bootleg records pressed on discarded x-rays - The Vinyl Factory

In the 1950s, Soviet citizens creatively circumvented music censorship by making bootleg records of American music on discarded X-ray plates from hospitals. They offer a fascinating glimpse into the underground music scene in Soviet Russia at the time.

Assessing the Dynamic Behavior of GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 Over Time

This study found that the performance of advanced AI models like GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 can change significantly over time, with their accuracy in completing tasks varying greatly. This research underscores the need for ongoing monitoring to ensure these AI systems remain reliable and effective in responding to user needs.
