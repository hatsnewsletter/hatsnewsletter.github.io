Analemmagraph - mitxela.com

- The author is interested in timelapses and specifically mentions solargraphs, which are six-month exposures with a pinhole camera to track the path of the sun. - Digital sensors have limited dynamic range, making it difficult to capture a clear image of the sun in a single shot. The author suggests using multiple cameras with different ND filters to create a super-HDR image. - Analemmas, the characteristic figure-8 shape of the sun's position at the same time each day throughout the year, can be plotted by extracting images at a specific time each day and stacking them together. - The author experiments with simulating the position of the sun using computer software, specifically Blender, to create a virtual solargraph. They achieve a decent result but encounter challenges with rendering a full year's worth of images and mapping the fisheye projection. - The author then creates an "analemmagraph" by replacing the sun with 365 individual miniature suns, representing each day of the year. They address the issues of atmospheric refraction and lack of weather by adding color effects and a random flicker. - The rendering engine is switched to Eevee for faster rendering times, although it has limitations with casting shadows from emissive objects. - The article concludes with a mention of further inaccuracies in the simulation.
