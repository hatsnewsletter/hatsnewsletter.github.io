Oasis: A Universe in a Transformer

Oasis is a real-time AI model that simulates gameplay in a virtual environment, trained on Minecraft. It can approximate mechanisms such as building/breaking blocks, and inventory management without a game engine. Its imperfect temporal coherence in long horizons adds a unique twist, allowing players to creatively reshape their surroundings.

Carpentopod: A walking table project

Inspired heavily by Theo Jansen’s “Strandbeest”, Gilliam de Carpentier turns the dynamic sculpture idea to a functional walking wooden coffee table. The design process involves a genetic algorithm to compare various mechanisms optimizing for minimum bobbing and foot slip. High-quality materials, such as CNC cut bamboo, and wireless control are two features not to miss.

Hideo Kodama’s significance in the history of 3D printing

Dr. Hideo Kodama pioneered 3D printing in 1981 by creating one of the first rapid prototyping machines using UV-curable resin. But nowadays Chuck Hull is often credited as the inventor for patenting stereolithography and developing the .stl format in 1986. The test print he used was a house, might be cool turn it into a benchmark model. The article explores the history of 3D printing chronologically.

Georg Nees: Computergrafik

Computer Graphics was the first global exhibition of algorithm-generated works, held at Siemens in Erlangen and the Studiengalerie der TH Stuttgart in February 1965. Georg Nees, the sole artist, displayed ten to twelve small graphics created by a programmed digital computer, pioneering computer art and advancing computer graphics technology.
