"Revolutionizing Motion Retargeting for Legged Robots with Differentiable Optimal Control”

The article by Disney Research discusses a new method for copying movements from animals or animations onto robots with legs, using a technique that makes these movements more accurate and efficient. This advancement is important for improving how robots move in fields like robotics, animation, and gaming.

Creative Technologies Talks -Aalto University

The Creative Technologies Talks hosted by Aalto University offer a series of videos featuring experts from universities and organizations such as KTH, Aalto University, and Imperial College London. These presentations delve into various aspects of creative technologies, including AI in music, performance, games, and art, as well as advancements in extended reality (XR) and computational design.

Is cat purring chaotic? -Not David

Not David investigates the science of cat purring, examining whether it is a chaotic or orderly process involving muscle, nerve, and brain coordination. It highlights the diversity in purring patterns among cats, linking this to chaos theory and its prevalence in many other phenomena.

Fun command line tools by Charm

Charm creates intuitive and elegant command line tools for activities such as sending emails, reading content, storing data, and terminal applications. They offer all these tools openly for public use.

Teardown Library: a community of product designers searching for inspiration

Teardown Library is a community-driven platform offering resources for product designers, including workshops, meetups, online forums, and design challenges. It provides hands-on experience by exploring various products, fostering skill improvement and networking opportunities for designers.

picoSYNTH by Johan Peitz

Created by Johan Peitz, PicoSYNTH makes creating simple loops and beats fun. The click and drag interface enables you to combine the equipment in interesting ways making your own analog synths!

USB-C cables head-to-head teardown by Lumafield

Lumafiels has CT scanned USB-C cables currently on the market. You can view the 3D CT scan data. They present their analysis and compare them against eachother.

Carl Bugeja’s Self-Reflowing Circuit Boards

Carl Bugeja shares his design and testing process of his self-soldering circuit boards. The PCB's ground plane layer is transformed into a resistor to heat up and melt solder, enabling the circuit to self-assemble and potentially create daughter circuits.

Crafting Miniature Magic: A Deep Dive into a 1:96 Scale Flying P-51 Mustang Model

Martin Newell crafted a remarkable 1:96 scale flying P-51 Mustang model, complete with features like navigation lights, flashing cannons, and retractable landing gear, all weighing just 2.9 grams and made mostly from scratch. Viewable on his YouTube channel.

Mixing 3D Printing + Fungi for Experimental Building Materials

PLP Architecture is pioneering in construction by combining 3D printing with fungi to create eco-friendly building materials. Their method involves using wood-based PLA molds infused with mycelium bio-composites, resulting in lightweight, fire-resistant materials ideal for soundproofing and insulation.

Soviet-era bootleg records pressed on discarded x-rays - The Vinyl Factory

In the 1950s, Soviet citizens creatively circumvented music censorship by making bootleg records of American music on discarded X-ray plates from hospitals. They offer a fascinating glimpse into the underground music scene in Soviet Russia at the time.

Assessing the Dynamic Behavior of GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 Over Time

This study found that the performance of advanced AI models like GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 can change significantly over time, with their accuracy in completing tasks varying greatly. This research underscores the need for ongoing monitoring to ensure these AI systems remain reliable and effective in responding to user needs.
