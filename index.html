- Apple's Thunderbolt 4 (USB-C) Pro cable supports Thunderbolt 3, Thunderbolt 4, and USB 4 data transfer with speeds up to 40 Gb/s and can deliver up to 100 W of power. - The Thunderbolt connector has a stainless steel enclosure and a strain relief crimped in from eight different directions. - The Thunderbolt cable has 24 pins on a 10-layer PCB board with blind and buried vias. - There is a wiggle detour on one of the traces to ensure the same length as its paired trace for high-speed data transmission. - The Thunderbolt cable contains different types of wires for charging and data transfer, including coaxially shielded conductors for high-speed data and unshielded wires for power and legacy USB 2.0 functionality. - Amazon Basics USB-C cable is much cheaper and offers charging up to 60 W and data transfer speeds up to 480 Mbps. - The Amazon Basics connector has two-armed strain relief and shielding anchored on the cable, with only 12 pins, four pairs of which are jumped across the PCBA. - The NiceTQ USB-C cable is a primitive cable with no metal shielding, a plastic enclosure strain relief, and eight pins, with only four connecting to the cable. - The NiceTQ cable has straightforward stranded conductors embedded in two layers of insulation, but it only supports USB 2.0 speeds. - The ATYFUER USB-C cable is a charging cable with a full set of 24 pins on a PCB, though only 12 are connected to traces and wires configured for U.
